# Daily Workflow

## When you apply to something

Open the job (or click **+ Add job** on the Jobs page if it is not already there), set the status to **Applied**, and fill in the application date. Set a **Follow-up date** about a week out so the dashboard nudges you if you have not heard back.

There is a quick button on the job page to set a follow-up a few business days out without editing the whole record.

## Finding and filtering jobs

On the Jobs page, click a column header to sort by it. Use the search box to filter by company or title, and use the status filter to narrow to a specific stage. Your sort choice and page-size preference are remembered between sessions.

## Bulk updates

Check the boxes next to multiple jobs and use the bulk action bar to change status or mark them as Withdrawn, Ghosted, or Pass across all of them at once. **Pass** is for postings you have decided not to pursue — it removes them from the default list view without deleting the records.

## The activity log

Every job keeps a running history. Status changes and follow-up date changes are logged automatically. You can add your own notes at any time from the job detail page. It is the easiest way to see what has happened with a role at a glance.

## Adding a contact and notes

On any job page, you can record the recruiter's name, email, and anything else you want to remember. Use the notes section for context about the role, the company, or anything from a conversation.

## Attaching files

On a job page, upload the exact resume and cover letter you used for that role. Choose the kind (Resume, Cover Letter, Job Description, Other) before uploading. That way you always know what version you sent, and the files travel with the job record.

## Interview debriefs

After every interview, write a debrief. On the job page, click **+ Add debrief** and fill in:

- the date and round type (Phone Screen, First Interview, etc.),
- the format (Phone, Video, In-person),
- how it felt (1 to 5),
- the interviewer's name,
- the questions they asked,
- what went well,
- what you would do better next time.

Writing debriefs immediately after each interview is one of the most valuable habits in the whole tool. Over a few interviews it shows patterns you can fix, and the notes become the basis for AI-generated interview prep guides if you use MCP mode.

You can also add a Google Calendar link from the debrief page to block the interview time on your calendar.

## Follow-up reminders

The dashboard shows every job where a follow-up is due (follow-up date is today or earlier and the job is still active). Recruiter follow-ups due appear separately in the same section.

When you follow up, clear or reset the follow-up date so the dashboard stays clean.

## Exporting

The **Export CSV** button on the Jobs page or Dashboard gives you the whole Job Squire as a spreadsheet. The Recruiters page has its own CSV export.
